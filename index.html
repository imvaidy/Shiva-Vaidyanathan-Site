( 04 ) FIELD NOTES

Writing, not just collecting.

Issue 02

The boutique is the easy part

Every quarter, a handful of niche and artisanal houses report growth numbers that would embarrass most mainstream FMCG brands — 60%, 90%, sometimes higher. The instinct is to read that as validation and open the next store. Opening the store is the easy part.

India rewards patience more than most markets. A market entry built on distribution alone plateaus fast. The houses that win India will be the ones that treat the first store as a beachhead for building real local category fluency, not a footnote on a global expansion slide.

Issue 01

Oud, sold two ways: Dubai and Singapore

In Dubai, oud is heritage first — a serious house can put agarwood front and center because the customer already knows what they're smelling. The sell is depth.

In Singapore, oud arrives as a discovery. The sell there is translation — without losing what makes it oud in the first place.
